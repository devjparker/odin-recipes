Cranberry

This is my own recipe for cranberry mead, inspired by my wife making cranberry sauce for Thanksgiving. The tartness of the cranberries really matches the sweet flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 gallon of water
  • 3 lbs of honey (about 12 cups)
  • 1 lb of cranberries
  • 3 oranges juiced and zested
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 sachet of wine yeast (alternatively can use bakers yeast, but it is more unpredictable)

Steps

  • Primary fermentation
    1. Bring your water to a boil
    2. Slowly pour in the honey while mixing
    3. Once all the honey has been added, let the water cool to about 100F
    4. Add the yeast and mix
    5. Put into fermenting container (carboy) for at least one month, at most until it clears and you can see through the liquid
    6. Re-rack for secondary and add in the cranberry sauce
  • When in secondary, make the cranberry sauce
    1. Heat the cranberries, orange juice, orange zest, and sugar
    2. The cranberries should naturally crush, but you may crush them yourself
    3. Once to a boil, let it simmer, then cool
    4. Add to the mead when cooled, and stir
  • You may want to monitor the acidity, as too much acid can kill yeast
  • After at least another week, at most a month, re-rack again, removing the cranberries and other particulates
  • Let sit until clear, then bottle or drink.
